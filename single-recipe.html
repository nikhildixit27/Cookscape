Masala Dosa

Masala dosa is a popular South Indian breakfast dish that consists of a crispy crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, and a savory filling of spiced potatoes. This delicious and filling dish is usually served with coconut chutney and sambar, a lentil soup with vegetables and spices.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : Breakfast lunch Snacks food

instructions

step 1

Rinse the idli rice and urad dal separately and soak them in enough water for at least 6 hours. Soak fenugreek seeds with the urad dal.

step 2

Drain the water and grind the rice and dal separately to a smooth batter using a wet grinder or a blender. Mix the rice and dal batters together and add salt as per taste. Mix well and leave the batter to ferment overnight or for 8 hours.

step 3

For the masala filling, heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida. Once the mustard seeds start to crackle, add chopped onions and green chilies. Fry till the onions turn translucent.

step 4

Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Adjust salt as per taste.

step 5

Heat a non-stick dosa tawa or a griddle. Once the tawa is hot, pour a ladleful of dosa batter on the center of the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook till the edges turn golden brown.

step 6

Place 2-3 tbsp of the masala filling on one half of the dosa and fold it over the other half. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

ingredients

2 cups of idli rice

1/2 cup flax seed meal

1 tsp fenugreek seeds

Water as needed

Salt to taste

4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed

1 onion, chopped

2 green chilies, finely chopped

1 tsp mustard seeds

1 tsp cumin seeds

tools

1 tsp cumin seeds

Non-stick dosa tawa or griddle

Ladle

Spatula
